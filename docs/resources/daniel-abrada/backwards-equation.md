# The Backwards Equation — Daniel Abrada

Source: transcript (2026-03-28)

## Overview

How to achieve anything by ignoring reality. Covers the backwards equation, seeing the equation, flipping the variables, holding the inversion through the lag.

## The Default Equation (Running Backwards)

Most people run: Circumstances → Thoughts → Feelings → Actions → Same Circumstances (loop)

- External event triggers automatic thought
- Thought produces feeling (tightness, anxiety, heaviness)
- You act from that feeling
- Actions produce more of the same reality
- Loop repeats without awareness

The whole sequence runs on autopilot. By the time you notice the thought, you've already reacted. The equation has already run.

**Key insight:** You're letting the output of your life dictate the input. When you do that, you're locked into producing the same result every time. Your reactions are reinforcing the very reality you're trying to change.

## Current Reality = Old Data

Your current circumstances are a printout — the result of an equation solved weeks, months, or years ago by the thoughts you were thinking and feelings you were carrying at that time.

- There is a built-in delay between internal change and external change
- Judging progress by current visible reality = always looking at evidence from an older version of yourself
- Current reality isn't a verdict — it's the tail end of an older process
- The equation that produced it has already been solved — you're free to write a new one

**The shift:** Stop fixing the output. Change the input. That's the only thing that moves the needle.

## Step 1: Seeing the Equation (The Equation Check)

Before you can flip anything, you have to see what's happening. The default equation runs below conscious awareness.

**The practice:** Pause a few times daily and ask: "Am I reacting to my reality right now, or am I creating it?"

- Catch the thought mid-flight = interrupt a compounding process
- Each interruption weakens the automaticity of the default equation
- Results are cumulative — easy to underestimate day-to-day

**Background apps analogy:** Recurring problems are like background apps on your phone. They consume energy silently. Every time you open one (give it attention + emotional investment), you tell the system "keep this active." Stop opening the app — eventually it times out.

**Key distinction:** This isn't suppressing feelings. It's developing the ability to notice a thought rising and choose whether to invest in it or let it pass.

- Consistency > intensity (3-4 brief checks throughout the day beats one morning meditation + 16 hours of autopilot)
- The default equation fires all day, so awareness practice must be distributed across the day
- "Between stimulus and response, there is a space — and in that space is your freedom and your power to choose"

**Why it's hard:** Your nervous system is built to read reality first, respond second. Survival mechanism. You're asking it to operate counterintuitively. Expect resistance. Be patient — catch more reactions over time on average. The trend matters infinitely more than any single instance.

## Step 2: Flipping the Variables (Morning Calibration)

The inverted equation: Thoughts → Feelings → Actions → New Circumstances

You choose the thought first, which generates the feeling. You create the internal state deliberately. Reality reorganizes around that.

**Why it works neurologically:** Your brain cannot tell the difference between a vividly felt internal experience and one actually happening externally. Mental rehearsal activates many of the same neural pathways as actual experience. Your biology doesn't fact-check the source.

**The morning practice (5 minutes, before phone):**
- Sit with a version of you who already has what you want
- NOT visualization of objects (mansion, car, body)
- Think and feel INTO a state — the internal experience of being that person
- The particular combination of thoughts and feelings present if your desired reality was already current
- Could be: deep calm + certainty, warm confidence, quiet gratitude + excitement
- Must be FELT in the body — not a picture in the mind or repeated affirmation
- Whole body experience = signal strong enough for nervous system to respond

**Compounding:** Single session feels nice but changes little. Daily for weeks fundamentally shifts baseline state. Small deposits add up. People who stick with it for months describe results as feeling "sudden" even though the process was gradual.

**Core principle:** The thoughts and feelings are the actual variables that determine the output. Money, relationships, health, opportunities — all downstream of an internal state held consistently enough for reality to reorganize around it.

## Step 3: Holding the Inversion Through the Lag (Lag Reframe)

When you shift internally, external reality doesn't update immediately. The gap between input change and output change is where almost everyone gives up.

**Why the lag exists:**
- Years of momentum running the old equation in one direction
- Momentum doesn't stop the moment you decide to change
- Old output was already in the pipeline, already queued up
- Nothing you do today can recall it or speed up the transition

**The lag reframe technique:** When old reality pushes back:
1. Recognize it for what it is — old output, already finished, already irrelevant
2. Don't engage with it
3. Bring attention back to the internal state from Step 2
4. Reconnect with the feeling/thoughts even for 10-15 seconds
5. Let the old output pass through without giving it energy

**Amazon analogy:** You pay, money leaves your account, but the package hasn't arrived yet. That delay doesn't mean the order didn't go through.

**The right question during the lag:** Not "Has my reality changed yet?" (keeps you focused on output). Instead: "Have I changed?" (the only metric within your control and the only one that predicts outcome)

**Each return to chosen state after disruption strengthens the inversion** — proves to your nervous system that this state can survive contact with contradictory evidence. Builds resilience. Each subsequent disruption becomes easier.

## The Three Steps as a System

1. **Equation Check** (distributed throughout day) — awareness to catch the default equation
2. **Morning Calibration** (5 min, before phone) — set the new input
3. **Lag Reframe** (as needed) — maintain new input when old output arrives

Each step supports the other two. Together they replace willpower with a system that keeps the new input flowing even on difficult days.

## Key Principles Summary

- Your current reality is a printout of a previous internal state
- When the internal state changes, the printout has to change — the equation has to balance
- The shift from fixing output to changing input is the whole game
- Thoughts and feelings come first, reality follows
- The lag between internal change and external change is built-in, not a sign of failure
- Hold the new input steady until the equation balances — that's your only job
