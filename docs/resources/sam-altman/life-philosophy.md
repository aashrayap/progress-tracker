# Life Philosophy

Source: Sam Altman (OpenAI founder)

## Understanding Yourself

- You don't have direct access to reality—no one does.
  Your brain constructs reality from bits of incoming information. What you perceive is a simulation crafted by your mind. Remarkably, you have the power to shape this simulation into either a heaven or a hell.

- Your beliefs shape your capabilities.
  "Whether you think you can or can't do something, you're right."

- Lack of understanding causes suffering.
  This refers to spiritual, not scientific, knowledge. We suffer by maintaining mental models that differ from reality. Seeing things as they are is liberating, like winning without fighting.

- You can give yourself unconditional love, and it's delightful.
  Self-love doesn't require you to change, nor does it demand achievement. You deserve it just as you are, and having it won't diminish your productivity.

## Understanding Others

- Conflict is mostly the result of people not being understood—by others and often by themselves.
  Understanding another person is one of the greatest gifts you can give because, without it, a person is like a lonely island in an endless sea.

- When we misbehave, we're often simply lost.
  Our perception of "badness" in others often stems from not accepting parts of ourselves that mirror the qualities we judge in them.

- Life is longer than a single project.
  The people you work with and work around are likely to meet you again in totally different circumstances. Spread good karma (that doesn't mean you shouldn't let go of underperforming people).
