# The Four Levels of Importance — Matthew Hussey

A framework for evaluating what you actually have with someone, beyond chemistry.

## Core Thesis

Picking a life partner is the most consequential decision you'll make — it shapes finances, parenting, health management, everything. Most people are bad judges of what will make them happy long-term because what grabs attention initially has little connection to future happiness. This four-level model cuts through that.

## The Four Levels

### Level 1: Admiration
- You find someone attractive, intriguing, impressive
- Could be someone on social media, at a party, a celebrity
- They may not know you exist
- **Not very important** — one-sided by definition

### Level 2: Mutual Attraction
- You've made a connection and they like you back
- Feels massively important because mutual chemistry in modern dating feels rare
- **The trap**: we stop being objective because the stakes feel impossibly high
- We become frightened to mess it up and stop thinking straight
- This level cannot tell you if someone would be great inside a relationship or is even capable of one

### Level 3: Commitment
- Both people say yes — not maybe, not "work is busy," not someday
- Enthusiastic, consistent commitment from both sides
- **Still not enough** — many committed couples fail because commitment alone doesn't mean it works

### Level 4: Compatibility
- Values and morality align
- Lifestyles work together
- You want the same futures
- **This is what determines if it actually works**
- Chemistry tells you there's a spark; compatibility asks if your lives fit together

## The Chemistry Traps

### Anxiety Masquerading as Chemistry
- The highs, lows, waiting, chasing, euphoria from getting just enough to stay hooked
- If someone feels more exciting when they pull away — that's your nervous system getting hijacked, not attraction
- Hussey's own experience: anytime he felt certain about someone, it had nothing to do with how right they were and everything to do with how much they triggered his insecurity
- "I wasn't certain they were right for me. I was just certain I had to have them, even if it cost me my voice"

### Confusing Impressive with Important
- Commands a room, charismatic at parties, impressive job that friends admire
- Not bad qualities, but: will this help when you're sick with a crying baby? When you've lost your job? When you're dealing with illness?
- "High standards" often means super picky on superficial traits while having zero standards for emotional safety, being treated well, or someone showing up on hard days

## Can Chemistry Grow with a Compatible Person?

**Unlikely to grow if**: you feel repelled, chronically bored, don't respect them, or physical attraction is entirely absent

**Can grow when you**:
- Stop confusing anxiety with chemistry
- Detox from the synthetic chemistry of emotional scarcity
- Make space for surprising forms of attraction — quiet confidence, seeing someone in their element
- This kind of person might not make you more *excited* but make you feel *better* than you ever have

## Settling For vs Settling On (revisited)

- Settling *for* = shortchanging yourself
- Settling *on* = acknowledging you could stay in the dating pool forever but consciously choosing to invest in someone awesome and build something extraordinary
- They may not be who your younger self wanted — "but what the hell did they know?"

## The Friend Test

- Think of a friend you love deeply
- If you introduced them to a stranger, that stranger might be underwhelmed
- But you know their history, what they've overcome, what makes them rich and beautiful as a person
- You know that because you *invested* over time until you genuinely saw their depth
- Your love life can work the same way

## Key Quotes

- "Love is not all you need. And it doesn't conquer all. Compatibility does."
- "Many more people are capable of being the person we can settle on and build something extraordinarily beautiful with"
- "The most profound beauty is to be created in what we build with that human being"
- "I wasn't certain they were right for me. I was just certain I had to have them, even if it cost me my voice"
