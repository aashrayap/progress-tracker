# Settling On vs Settling For — Matthew Hussey

Why modern dating feels harder despite infinite options, and why investment creates meaning.

## Core Thesis

Technology gave us unlimited choice but made choosing harder. The paradox of choice creates overwhelm, lower investment, and perpetual optimization that prevents deep bonding. The answer isn't finding the perfect person — it's settling *on* someone with good fundamentals and building something extraordinary together.

## The Paradox of Choice in Dating

- Endless options create lower investment in any single person
- We divide attention across potentials waiting for certainty before committing
- Even after choosing, we think about what we didn't choose
- The buffet mentality ("I'll invest fully once I find the perfect one") guarantees we never invest fully

## Oliver Berkman's 4000 Weeks Parallel

- We live with the fantasy we'll do it all — a denial of how short life is
- Enjoyment comes from picking a tiny handful of things and resolving to enjoy them deeply
- Striving and investing in something creates maximum enjoyment, not trying to experience everything
- Applied to love: the desire to experience all options or have the perfect love life makes it impossible to actually choose

## Fundamentals Framework

### Universal Fundamentals (hard to argue against in any relationship)
- Teamwork
- Kindness and compassion
- Ability to argue well and communicate effectively
- Showing up for each other in difficult times
- Making space for each other's flaws

### Personal Fundamentals (legitimately non-negotiable for you)
- Vary by individual — example: physical affection if you grew up in an affectionate family
- The test: would its absence create long-term unhappiness regardless of everything else?
- Not universal — two people who don't need physical affection won't miss it together

### Nice-to-Haves Disguised as Fundamentals
- Specific body type, height, status markers
- Things that feel essential but dissolve after deep bonding
- The danger: disqualifying people based on these prevents ever reaching the bonding threshold

## The Bonding Threshold

There's a before and after of deep connection:
- **Before**: many superficial things feel like dealbreakers
- **After**: those things dissolve; what occupies your thoughts is what you truly treasure
- The danger: we disqualify people before ever crossing this line, so we never learn what actually matters to us on the other side

## The Kaizen Warning

- Never-ending improvement is wonderful *inside* a relationship
- It is poison in the *selection* process
- Applied to choosing: every person with many right things has one wrong thing, so you go searching for someone with all those right things *plus* the missing one
- Result: you trade one sub-optimal thing for a different sub-optimal thing, endlessly
- The fantasy partner who ticks every box does not exist

## Settling For vs Settling On

| | Settling *for* | Settling *on* |
|---|---|---|
| Implies | Shortchanging yourself, throwing in the towel | Conscious, powerful decision to invest |
| Pride lives in | The initial choice | What you build together |
| Source of meaning | The person *is* the one | You *create* the one-ness together |

- Choosing someone and investing creates something that didn't exist before — that's alchemy
- You can retroactively say "this is the one" because you built something one-of-a-kind together
- Meaning and fulfillment flow where investment goes
- History can only be made by something you spend time on

## The Postmates Analogy

- Starving hungry, confronted with 10 cuisines he adores
- If any one had just shown up, he'd have been happy
- But seeing all options simultaneously made him unhappy trying to optimize
- You can't get full on 10 cuisines in one night — you have to choose and be present with that meal
- Same FOMO applies to people in relationships fantasizing about alternatives

## Applies to People Already in Relationships

- Dissatisfaction or boredom triggers fantasizing about what's outside
- Often what they find isn't an upgrade in person — just a temporary change in state
- Short-term excitement of novelty, but stasis meets them again in the new relationship
- Recipe: reclassify what your fundamentals actually are, try to bring them out in the current relationship, then decide with clarity

## Key Quotes

- "The real self-actualization is two people realizing the true potential of a relationship by what they bring to it"
- "We value what we invest in — meaning and fulfillment flows where our investment goes"
- "People aren't customizable... the rewards come from the small graces and spaces we afford to allow for another autonomous human being in our life"
